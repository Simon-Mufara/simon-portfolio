Early Childhood Development

Oncology & Nephrology Internship at Reach For A Dream

Project Period: January 2023 - December 2023
Organization: Reach For A Dream, Johannesburg
Role: Early Childhood Development Intern (Oncology & Nephrology)

Overview

Served as an Early Childhood Development Intern focusing on oncology and nephrology wards at two major Johannesburg hospitals. Worked directly with healthcare practitioners to improve patient data management, deliver health education, and support families navigating serious childhood illnesses.

Key Responsibilities

  • Supervised pediatric wards at Chris Hani Baragwanath Hospital and Charlotte Maxeke Johannesburg Academic Hospital
  • Collaborated with over 30+ health practitioners including doctors, nurses, and social workers
  • Managed 200+ Electronic Medical Records using REDCap
  • Delivered 10+ structured health education sessions per month to children and families
  • Coordinated patient support services and family resources

Tools & Systems

REDCap Electronic Medical Records Data Management Health Education Patient Care Coordination

Hospital Facilities

Chris Hani Baragwanath Hospital

One of the largest hospitals in the world, serving the Soweto community:

  • Worked in pediatric oncology and nephrology wards
  • Supported high-volume patient care environment
  • Collaborated with multidisciplinary teams
  • Managed complex case documentation

Charlotte Maxeke Johannesburg Academic Hospital

Major tertiary academic hospital in central Johannesburg:

  • Assisted with specialized pediatric care
  • Supported teaching and training activities
  • Participated in ward rounds and case discussions
  • Coordinated between departments for comprehensive care

Electronic Medical Records Management

REDCap Implementation

Led the management of 200+ electronic medical records using REDCap (Research Electronic Data Capture):

  • Data Entry & Validation: Ensured accurate and complete patient information capture
  • Quality Control: Implemented validation rules to maintain data integrity
  • Accessibility Improvement: Organized data for easy retrieval by healthcare teams
  • Reporting: Generated patient summaries and progress reports
  • Compliance: Maintained patient confidentiality and HIPAA-equivalent standards

Impact on Healthcare Delivery

  • Reduced time spent searching for patient information
  • Improved continuity of care through better documentation
  • Enhanced communication between healthcare team members
  • Enabled better tracking of patient outcomes and treatment progress
  • Supported research and quality improvement initiatives

Health Education Programs

Monthly Education Sessions

Delivered 10+ structured health education sessions per month covering:

  • Disease Understanding: Age-appropriate explanations of cancer and kidney diseases
  • Treatment Education: What to expect during chemotherapy, dialysis, and other treatments
  • Symptom Management: How to recognize and respond to treatment side effects
  • Nutrition & Wellness: Dietary guidelines for children undergoing treatment
  • Emotional Support: Coping strategies for children and families
  • Home Care: Post-discharge care instructions and medication management

Target Audiences

  • Pediatric patients (age-appropriate materials and activities)
  • Parents and primary caregivers
  • Extended family members
  • Community health workers

Program Impact

200+ patients with improved medical record accessibility

120+ education sessions delivered over 12 months

30+ health practitioners collaborated with across two major hospitals

Improved data quality leading to better patient outcomes and care coordination

Collaboration with Healthcare Teams

Multidisciplinary Approach

Worked closely with diverse healthcare professionals:

  • Pediatric Oncologists: Supported cancer treatment planning and monitoring
  • Nephrologists: Assisted with kidney disease management
  • Nurses: Coordinated daily patient care activities
  • Social Workers: Connected families with community resources
  • Dietitians: Implemented nutritional support plans
  • Child Life Specialists: Facilitated age-appropriate coping activities

Skills Developed

  • Electronic health record management and data quality assurance
  • Health education curriculum development and delivery
  • Pediatric patient care and family support
  • Multidisciplinary team collaboration
  • Cultural sensitivity in healthcare delivery
  • Medical terminology and clinical documentation
  • Crisis communication and emotional support
  • Time management in high-pressure healthcare environments

Key Learnings

  • Importance of accurate medical documentation for patient safety
  • Value of family-centered care in pediatric oncology
  • Role of health education in improving treatment adherence
  • Impact of socioeconomic factors on health outcomes
  • Significance of compassionate communication with families facing serious illness

Personal Growth

This internship profoundly shaped my understanding of healthcare delivery in resource-constrained settings and reinforced my commitment to using data and technology to improve patient outcomes. Working with children facing life-threatening illnesses and their families taught me resilience, empathy, and the critical importance of clear communication in healthcare.
