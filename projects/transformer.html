Transformer-Based Multi-Omics Drug Response Prediction for African Breast Cancer

Project Period: January 2025 - December 2025
Institution: University of Cape Town
Role: Honours Bioinformatics Researcher

Overview

Developed an advanced transformer-based multi-omics model specifically designed to predict drug response in African breast cancer patients. This project addresses the critical underrepresentation of African populations in cancer research and personalized medicine.

Key Achievements

  • Applied transfer learning to fine-tune models on African-specific TNBC (Triple-Negative Breast Cancer) and HER2+ cohorts
  • Achieved improved accuracy compared to baseline models through population-specific training
  • Integrated 5+ major datasets including BCaPE, H3Africa, GEO, TCGA, and GDSC
  • Processed and analyzed over 1,000 patient samples
  • Successfully integrated multiple omics layers: gene expression, mutations, and methylation data

Technology Stack

PyTorch Transformers Python pandas scikit-learn NumPy matplotlib seaborn

Methodology

The project employed a comprehensive approach to multi-omics integration and machine learning:

  • Data Preprocessing: Normalized and harmonized data across multiple omics layers and datasets
  • Feature Engineering: Developed novel features for multi-modal data integration specific to breast cancer subtypes
  • Model Architecture: Designed transformer-based architecture utilizing attention mechanisms to capture complex omics interactions
  • Transfer Learning: Fine-tuned pre-trained models on African-specific cancer cohorts to improve prediction accuracy
  • Validation: Employed rigorous cross-validation and holdout test sets to ensure model generalizability

Data Integration

The project successfully integrated multiple large-scale datasets:

  • BCaPE (Breast Cancer in African Populations Epidemiology): African-specific breast cancer genomics data
  • H3Africa: Human Heredity and Health in Africa consortium data
  • GEO (Gene Expression Omnibus): Public gene expression repositories
  • TCGA (The Cancer Genome Atlas): Comprehensive cancer genomics data
  • GDSC (Genomics of Drug Sensitivity in Cancer): Drug response information

Clinical Impact

This research contributes significantly to personalized medicine for African breast cancer patients:

  • Enables prediction of drug efficacy before treatment administration
  • Addresses critical gaps in cancer research equity and representation
  • Provides foundation for population-specific treatment strategies
  • Potentially reduces treatment failures and improves patient outcomes
  • Creates framework for similar studies in other underrepresented populations

Technical Challenges & Solutions

  • Data Heterogeneity: Developed robust normalization pipelines to handle diverse data sources
  • Missing Data: Implemented advanced imputation techniques specific to omics data
  • Class Imbalance: Applied sophisticated resampling and weighting strategies
  • Computational Resources: Optimized model architecture for efficient training on available hardware

Research Significance

This project represents a crucial step toward equitable healthcare by ensuring that AI-driven precision medicine tools work effectively for African populations, who have historically been underrepresented in cancer genomics research.

Future Directions

  • Expand model to additional cancer subtypes
  • Incorporate additional omics layers (proteomics, metabolomics)
  • Collaborate with clinical partners for prospective validation
  • Develop user-friendly clinical decision support tools
