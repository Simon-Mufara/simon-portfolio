EH!Woza Science Workshops

Community Science Education & HIV/TB Awareness

Project Period: February 2025 - November 2025
Organization: EH!Woza
Role: Voluntary Facilitator

Overview

Serving as a voluntary facilitator for EH!Woza, an organization dedicated to improving science literacy and health awareness in township communities. Through interactive workshops and educational sessions, I work to inspire young students in underserved communities to pursue STEM education while providing crucial health information.

Key Activities

  • Facilitated 5+ interactive science workshops focusing on HIV/TB awareness
  • Designed monthly youth engagement sessions
  • Reached 50+ students per session
  • Created age-appropriate educational materials
  • Mentored students interested in STEM fields
  • Collaborated with community leaders and educators

Skills Utilized

Science Education Public Speaking Curriculum Design Community Engagement Youth Mentorship

Workshop Topics

HIV/AIDS Awareness

Comprehensive education on HIV/AIDS for young students:

  • Transmission & Prevention: How HIV spreads and how to prevent infection
  • Testing & Treatment: Importance of knowing your status and available treatments
  • Living with HIV: Dispelling myths and reducing stigma
  • Support Systems: Available resources and support networks
  • Future Outlook: Scientific advances in HIV treatment and prevention

Tuberculosis Education

Essential knowledge about TB transmission, treatment, and prevention:

  • What is TB: Understanding the disease and its impact
  • Symptoms & Diagnosis: Recognizing TB symptoms and importance of early detection
  • Treatment Adherence: Why completing TB medication is critical
  • Drug-Resistant TB: Understanding MDR-TB and XDR-TB
  • Prevention: Protecting yourself and your community

Basic Microbiology

  • Introduction to microorganisms and their role in disease
  • How vaccines work and why they're important
  • The immune system and how it protects us
  • Antibiotics and antimicrobial resistance
  • Hygiene practices to prevent disease spread

Public Health Principles

  • Understanding epidemics and pandemics
  • Community health and disease prevention
  • Healthcare access and health equity
  • Role of public health in society
  • Career opportunities in healthcare and public health

Teaching Methodology

Interactive Learning

Workshops employ hands-on, engaging activities:

  • Demonstrations: Visual and practical demonstrations of scientific concepts
  • Group Discussions: Facilitated conversations to explore topics deeply
  • Q&A Sessions: Open forums for students to ask questions
  • Activities & Games: Educational games that reinforce learning
  • Real-World Applications: Connecting science to daily life

Age-Appropriate Content

  • Tailored materials for different age groups
  • Use of visual aids, diagrams, and videos
  • Simple language avoiding medical jargon
  • Cultural sensitivity in content delivery
  • Relatable examples from community context

5+ workshops facilitated on HIV/TB awareness and science education

  • Demonstrated career pathways in STEM

  • Equipped students to share health information with families

Personal Reflection

Working with EH!Woza has been incredibly rewarding, allowing me to give back to communities similar to those I come from. Seeing students' faces light up when they understand a scientific concept or realize they could pursue a career in science is what drives this work. This experience has reinforced my belief that science education and health literacy are fundamental to community empowerment and social equity.

"The best way to predict the future is to create it, and education is the most powerful tool we have to build that future."

Through EH!Woza, I'm helping create a future where township youth have the knowledge, inspiration, and support to pursue their dreams in science and healthcare.
